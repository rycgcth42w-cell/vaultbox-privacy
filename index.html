VaultBox

Privacy Policy  ·  Effective date: June 1, 2025

📋 Overview

VaultBox is a time-locked encrypted vault app for macOS, developed by Aino. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and what leaves your device.

VaultBox is designed with privacy as a core principle. All your vault data is encrypted locally on your Mac and never sent to any server operated by the developer.

📦 Data Stored on Your Device

VaultBox stores the following data exclusively on your Mac:

  • Encrypted vault contents (files, notes, and items you add)
  • Locker names, icons, and timer configurations (via SwiftData)
  • Master password hash and per-locker encryption keys (via macOS Keychain)
  • SMTP credentials for emergency email alerts (via macOS Keychain, encrypted)
  • Emergency recipient email address (encrypted, via macOS Keychain)
  • ntfy.sh topic name (via UserDefaults – not sensitive)

None of this data is transmitted to the developer or any third-party analytics service.

🌐 Network Activity User-initiated only

VaultBox makes outgoing network connections only when you explicitly trigger an emergency alert. No background telemetry or analytics traffic is ever sent.

  • SMTP email — sent to your chosen recipient address using credentials you provide. VaultBox connects directly to the SMTP server you configure (e.g. smtp.gmail.com). No data passes through developer-operated servers.
  • ntfy.sh push notification — an alert message is posted to the ntfy.sh topic name you configure. This is an optional feature. See ntfy.sh's privacy policy for details on their service.

🚫 What We Never Do

  • We never collect analytics, crash reports, or usage statistics
  • We never access your vault contents — all encryption happens on-device
  • We never share any data with advertisers or data brokers
  • We never store anything on developer-operated servers
  • We never use tracking technologies of any kind

🔑 macOS Permissions Used

  • App Sandbox — VaultBox runs in a sandbox for your security
  • Network access — only for user-triggered emergency alerts (SMTP / ntfy.sh)
  • File access (user-selected) — to let you import files into your vault
  • Notifications — to alert you when a timer-locked locker becomes accessible
  • Keychain — to securely store encryption keys and credentials

👶 Children's Privacy

VaultBox is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children.
